Pythagoras Theorem -

Detailed Explanation Introduction

The Pythagoras Theorem is one of the most fundamental concepts in mathematics, especially in geometry. It establishes a relationship between the sides of a right-angled triangle. Named after the ancient Greek mathematician Pythagoras, this theorem helps us calculate the length of one side of a triangle when the other two are known.

Statement of the Theorem

In a right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse (the side opposite the right angle) is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides.
